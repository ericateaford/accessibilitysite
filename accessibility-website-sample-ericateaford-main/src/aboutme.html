    When I hear Accessibility

    My name is Erica Teaford and I am a senior at Weber State University located in Ogden, UT. My major is Web and User Experience Design with an emphasis in UX Design.

    When I hear accessibility I think how imporatnt it is to make a webpage accessible so that everyone can use the site. I think of people who are like me, who sometimes struggle to focus on a task at once before their attention is snagged away almost instaneously. I also think of the individuals who may have a physical disabiliity that may prevent them from effectively browsing the web. With the sudden emergence of technoology, and as it grows exponentially it is my responsibility that users with disabilities can not just access the web, but be able to easily navigate it. The internet is a new and ever expanding world, and everyone deserves to be able to navigate it.

    Automated Tools are NOT Enough

    Automated tools are not enough when designing websites with acessibility in mind. By adding to automated tools(ex: provided a description for alt text for an image), we are allowing our users with disabilities to have a deeper understanding of our website and what it can truly offer our users. Instead of a robotic experience if we just used automated tools, we are allowing a connection between or company and our users. They will feel welcomed when naivgating the webpage because we took time to think of them in the web designing process.

    Measures to support accessibility

    ORGANIZATION takes the following measures to ensure accessibility of Teaford Edu: Include accessibility as part of our mission statement. Provide continual accessibility training for our staff. Assign clear accessibility goals and responsibilities. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. Teaford Edu is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.
